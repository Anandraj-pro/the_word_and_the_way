## 2. What went well

- **Reuse over rebuild.** `EncounterCard` carried 4-2, 4-3, and 4-4; the `season_id` filter, the `DELETE /encounters/{id}` endpoint, and `/on-this-day` all pre-existed and only needed surfacing.
- **Recon-before-scope.** 4-1 revealed the season spine/lens was already built freehand; scoping to the genuine gap (weight + current-marking) instead of rebuilding kept the story honest. create-story's exhaustive analysis earned its keep.
- **Multi-layer review precision.** The Edge Case Hunter (code access) repeatedly refuted the Blind Hunter's speculative findings with grounded reads — the `n≥1` guarantee (4-4), the count-vs-list "divergence" (4-2). High signal, low noise.
- **The room stayed calm.** A whole archive + library + remembrance added with no routed pages and no blocking `confirm()` dialogs — accordions and inline confirms only.

## 3. What was hard — the real lessons

- **Ripple effects / blast radius.** 4-2's mount-gate relaxation silently broke the room tour (caught in review); 4-3's delete had to route through `Room.load()` or it would desync the 4-1 spine weights. Changes to Room wiring have reach.
- **Robustness under-handled on first pass.** New interactivity shipped with gaps caught in review: 4-3's fire-and-forget delete (no await/guard/catch) and 4-4's content-less row (all-null encounter). Predictable enough to front-load.
- **Build-as-verification ceiling.** No test runner, so pure, easily-testable logic (`yearsAgo`/`keptOn` date math, the verse-filter regex, `weight()`) is verified only by reasoning. 4-4's populated path couldn't be observed without seeded data.
